## The #2825 arc — worth recording in full

#2814 merged at 23:40 and the very next main CI run failed
`ws-server-stdio-load.spec.ts` at its 12s budget. A QA session called it
"pre-existing flaky"; the run timeline said otherwise (green at 23:38,
first red = the #2814 merge commit, test exercises the changed file).

- **Round 1** (unframed nerd-snipe): deterministic repro of a result-line
  drop — `proc.exited` judged completion before stdout drained; #2814's new
  guard dropped the buffered `result` line. Fix PR #2830: await the drain
  before judging. review:pass; QA proved causal necessity by revert-check.
- **The halt**: #2830's own CI failed the same signature on the fixed tree.
  Per the one-repair escalation rule I halted to needs-attention, posted the
  timeline, asked the human (away), took the recommended default: second
  investigation, no repairs.
- **Round 2**: REFUTED the "drain too slow under contention" hypothesis with
  measurements (all 8×512KiB sessions drain ≤365ms at load average 340) and
  proved a second trigger by A/B: the test's bare-`cat` fixture exits
  without reading stdin → EPIPE on the daemon's initial prompt write →
  failSend → disconnect *before the reader wires* → same guard drop.
  8/10 stalls with `cat`, 0/10 with `sh -c 'cat "$1"; cat >/dev/null'`.
  A test-double defect with no production analog for that write.
- **Resolution**: fixture fix on the same PR (N/bytes/12s budget untouched),
  delta-reviewed, fresh QA, CI green first try. The four held PRs
  branch-updated, relabeled with mechanism citation, all merged within 6
  minutes. Guard-narrowing defense-in-depth filed as #2838 so the *class*
  (any premature disconnect drops buffered output) gets one structural fix
  instead of per-trigger patches.

## What worked well

- **The escalation discipline paid for itself twice.** The halt looked
  pedantic (a mechanism-verified fix, red "only" on a known signature) —
  and was right: the red on the fixed tree was a genuinely different bug.
  Rerun-until-green would have merged #2830 and left the fixture landmine
  for every future PR.
- **Unframed investigations with measurement mandates.** Round 2 was told
  to verify a specific hypothesis and refuted it with instrumented numbers
  instead of confirming the frame it was handed. That refusal-to-confirm is
  what surfaced the fixture defect.
- **Worker pushback on a wrong orchestrator instruction.** #2804's impl
  session (Hank) refused my round-2 revert order, produced an edit-status
  controlled hash comparison, and cited CI's clean-checkout `phase-lock`
  pass. He was right; QA-2's verification tool had resolved the base
  checkout via #2737 and validated the wrong tree. The qa:fail override
  rule (read the artifacts, name the mechanism, independent verification)
  turned a he-said-she-said into a 5-minute resolution.
- **The sprint's own deliverables caught two P1s in-flight**: #2797's
  isolation exposed #2821 the moment the smoke test stopped lying;
  #2800's anchored pattern kept the build smoke honest under it.
- **Batching + blockedBy edges held**: zero hot-file collisions; the
  #2800→#2801 and #2761→#2760/#2771 serializations played out exactly as
  planned; amendment gate sequenced #2821 behind #2822 cleanly.
- **#2744's verify-before-implement gate** saved a full impl slot on an
  issue whose fix had merged 19 days prior.

## Patterns established

- **Held-PR cascade**: when N PRs are red only on one main-level regression,
  fix at main once, then per-PR: update-branch (fresh merge ref), flip
  qa:fail→qa:pass with the mechanism cited, arm auto-merge — merge happens
  only if the rerun is genuinely green. All four landed in one pass.
- **Delta review**: for an escalated PR gaining a commit after round-1
  approval, review the delta commit explicitly (round-2 reviewer told
  exactly which commit was already approved) instead of re-reviewing the
  world.
- **Override protocol validated**: qa:fail overrides carried independent
  verification (base-vs-branch grep + CI phase-lock log) and named the
  mechanism in the comment, both times they were used.
