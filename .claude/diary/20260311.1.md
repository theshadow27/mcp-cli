### PR #618 — Well-known WebSocket port (#611, P1)

Repair added retry-with-backoff (5 attempts, 200ms), input validation [0,65535], and proper fixed-port test.

## What worked well

1. **Adversarial review depth on #597** — two full rounds caught 17 real issues. The second round found critical issues the first missed (in-process eval blocking event loop, console.log corrupting subprocess protocol). Worth the cost for a 946-line rewrite.
2. **Triage accuracy** — 0 false negatives again. Low-scrutiny PRs all merged clean. High-scrutiny PRs all had real findings.
3. **Throughput** — 24 PRs in ~2.5 hours wall time. 5 opus impl slots + unlimited sonnet review/QA kept pipeline saturated.
4. **First automated release** — v0.2.0 tagged by GHA on first try after fixing 4 blockers in review.

## What didn't work

1. **Worktree cleanup killed an active session** — cleaned 190 worktrees in bulk, accidentally destroyed #611's worktree. Had to respawn. Should check active sessions before bulk cleanup.
2. **#591 first attempt didn't PR** — session did the work but ended without pushing. Had to retry. Unclear why — possibly the 1-line change made it think no PR was needed.
3. **Daemon restart killed QA session** — `mcx shutdown` after rebuild orphaned the #597 QA session. Should wait for active sessions to complete before restarting.

## Patterns established

- **Two adversarial review rounds for rewrites** — one round catches the obvious issues, second round catches issues introduced by the fixes. 17 findings across 2 rounds on #597 validates this.
- **Don't bulk-clean worktrees during a sprint** — check `mcx claude ls` first.
- **Rebuild + restart between batches, not mid-batch** — wait for all active sessions to idle before `mcx shutdown`.
- **Tag-triggered releases > push-to-main releases** — avoids race conditions, infinite loops, and protected branch issues.
